< GENERAL GEO. INFORMATION'S>

Canary islands are situated at 28º parallel north and 15º meridian east. Northwest coast of African continent is on closest point about 100 kilometers away. The archipelago is made from seven main islands and four small, and they are:

Gran Canaria
Tenerife
La Palma
La Gomera
Lanzarote
Fuerteventura
El Hiero

The four small islands are: La Graciosa, Montaña Clara, Alegranca and Lobos.

The Canarias made one part of archipelago together with Azores, Madeira & Cabo Verde. They are all with volcanic origin and similar ecosystem. There is many different theory trying to explain origin of islands, but volcanic theory is most accepted and fact of life volcanos (last eruption en La Palma, 1971), support theory about growing Atlantic ocean.

Most visible fact on Canary relief is altitude (Tenerife, 3718 met.). From where begins many canyons, deep sometimes more than 1000 met. Coastal parts are very high in eastern islands and on western is more flat with long sand beaches. All islands have central orientation of altitude and canyons that finish in ocean. Only Lanzarote, Fuerteventura & south of Gran Canaria have sand extensions
