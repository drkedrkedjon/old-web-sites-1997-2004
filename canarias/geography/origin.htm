<THE BIRTH OF ISLANDS>

Modern technology has discover some unknown aspects of the geological past of Archipelago. Now is known that Gran Canaria was the third island born from the Atlantic Ocean. Island current shape is the result of three volcanic eruptions which occurred throughout millions of years.

The first one was happened between fourteen and nine million years ago, which create Caldera de Tejeda, probably mother crater of the island.

The second eruption occurred in the same high region, between three and a half and four and a half million years ago. Island increased in size considerably. Rivers of lava had filled the hollows formed on much of the island by the effects of the previous volcano. This formation "Roque Nublo" is Canarian name that has been accepted by volcanists around the world.

The third cycle, occurred eight hundred thousand years ago and continued until three thousand years ago. It determined the current geography of Gran Canaria shaped and re - shaped by the breaking waves and meteors whose action endangers the beautiful monoliths, such as the Dedo de Dios (God's Finger) in Agaete.

Island is separated on two parts one is Tamaran or Old Island. The second is New Island. The largest rifts are situated on the central slope. The amazing sight of these rifts, Pechos de la Cumbre and Pozo de las Nieves, the highest point of the island (1950 m). Deep cliffs such as those at Tejeda, Siberio or Tirajana with its enormous crater, monoliths known as Nublo and Bentaiga: the mountain range of Aserrador an apocalyptic vision that looks like a storm turned to stone.

171 volcanos have been found in New Island.
