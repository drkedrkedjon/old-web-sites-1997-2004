<ABORIGINES, THE ANCIENT HABITANTS, PART 2>

Housing...

The geological structure of islands have determined the way of living and housing. Excluding Fuerteventura and Lanzarote, where they use volcano pipes, Aborigines lived in natural caves. Also with natural caves they use artificial caves. We can found presents of artificial caves mostly in Gran Canaria. They are oval caves with more than one rooms. Some form of windows are also in use. Many of caves have geometrical paintings on walls. On Gran Canaria could be found also some housing on superficies. Oval houses with one or more rooms made from rocks without any cementing.

Politics...

On Gran Canaria civil political institutions was Guanarteme, elected from nobles close to dynasty. Religion were in hands of Faycan, member of Guanarteme. Besides those were two more. One civil made from: Guanarteme, Faycan and different nobles, who had function of communicating with king about questions regarding government and justice. Second was military named Sabor, and is possible that functionality was only in times of crisis.

Economy...

On all islands, except Gran Canaria, basis de economy was pastoral. Cabra, sheep and pig were present. Agriculture is on secondary position. On opposite, Gran Canaria and north de Tenerife were agricultural with high technical development. Also fruits and coastal fishing bring goods to people. In the field of production is present ceramic, but mostly made by family for they necessities, wood industry is present very little, production of tolls is made on basis of rocks, because there is no metal on islands. At end, of course, many products made from fibre vegetal and leather.
