<LOCAL WEATHER SPECIALTY>

There is few local specialties when we talk about clime in Canary islands. First is wind that comes from influence of anticyclone in Azores. It is always present, in same direction and in constant power, depending on season, that make this island "the place" for windsurfing. Of course wind have more important role because it brings heavy humidity from Atlantic ocean that condense in mountains.

Humidity, pushed by wind in upper levels of island mountains, fall on land in form of "horizontal rain". This is second specialty of Canary islands Horizontal rain is mostly present in autumn and winter and mostly in north of island. Sometimes when anticyclone goes more on south it bring cold weather and then normally could rain.

Secondly, when anticyclone goes more on north it brings "Sirocco or Calima", nasty wind from Sahara with sand and no humidity at all. It brings temperature sometimes up to 40ºC.

And maybe the last specialty is diversity of clime in one island. You can drive around island in one day and change all weathers situation in few hours. You will catch small rain in north, fog and maybe even snow in mountains and sunny beaches in south. You newer know... but it can be great experience as you drive around.
