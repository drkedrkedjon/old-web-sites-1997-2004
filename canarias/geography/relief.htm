<RELIEF ON ISLAND>

After the island had emerged from the ocean birds are first living creatures that came on this isolated and deserted island. Carried by the wind the seafaring birds build their nests or rested here, Different natural mechanisms were to influence the islands vegetation. Some seeds have been carried by birds.

Another way for the seeds to get to the Canaries is by fruits that floated and ended on island shores. Normally most of the seeds are lost however island has been today cowered with plants from different parts of the world.

The oldest plants are the "verodes" and "tajinastes". Dragon trees and milk thistles are the second oldest, originating in east and south Africa. Laurel barbuzano till, can still be found. The Canarian pine tree, that arrived in Gran Canaria at the end of the tertiary age is according to botanists also found in the Himalayas. Canarian laurels arrived island at the end of the tertiary age.

Due to the excellent climatic conditions it found here this vegetation has remained unchanged whilst in the places it originated in, it was transformed or lost forever Agave. Escobones and retamas (types of plant) grow around the medium heights and peaks, by the forests of pinus canariensis. They arrived on Gran Canaria through the Atlas Mountains. From the Mediterranean. Gorse and tarajales are two of the plants that appeared recently. Which originated in the Sahara. The different varieties of mint whose aromatic and medicinal leaves are so pleasant, come from southern Africa and South America whose closest relative is a herb found in the Himalayas and western China. There is 242 plants which are endemic to Gran Canaria, and are considered unique in the world.

The animal population here is a quite mystery. Animals arrived here either blown by the wind, flying by their own means, or using vegetation as rafts. Insects, birds and bats form part of the first and second groups. The last group covers animals brought by man and includes the green frog from south east Europe and north Africa, currently in danger of extinction. The lizard world is of particular interest in the Canary Islands. One of the species. According to fossil remains found on the western islands is 1 0 million years old The lizards living today are between 0.50 and 0.60 meters in length but can measure up to one meter. They are not dangerous, only eating plants. The bird world is represented by 61 nesting species. However, many others are migratory, stopping here regularly on their journey to other lands or spending the winter here. A third group of birds is made up of those who are rare or occasional visitors to the islands. One of their favorite places to stop and break up was the Charca de Maspalomas. Even today you can still spot many of them on this comfortable aquatic orchard.
